1. Lost in Conversion: the loss ledgerconversion-machinery

Description: Trace one real skill through the converter pipeline and publish the ledger of what each platform confiscates: Kiro rewrites every surgical Edit into a full-file write and clips names at 64 chars on a hyphen boundary; Copilot warns (never blocks) past 30k chars; OpenCode's deep-merge lets the user's existing config win on every key; Codex ships dual-mode install purely to avoid double-registration. Framed with translation theory: untranslatable words, abridgment with a translator's note, localization vs translation.

Basis: direct: src/converters/claude-to-kiro.ts:25 "Edit: 'write', // NOTE: Kiro write is full-file, not surgical edit. Lossy mapping." · src/targets/opencode.ts:33-36 spread order (incoming first, existing last) · Copilot 30k warning in claude-to-copilot.ts. Verifier-confirmed verbatim.

Rationale: The converter is the only major subsystem no published piece touches, and five of five ideation frames independently proposed it — "9 platforms from one source" is the plugin's most marketable structural fact, and the honest per-target loss ledger is the version no marketing page writes.

Downsides: Needs a real conversion run per target for the trace; three targets are unfinished, so the ledger has gaps to disclose.

Confidence: 90% · Complexity: Medium

Closing beat: git shows what raced, but why it won't recur lives in files the article's own constraint forbids opening — the thesis about where knowledge lives.

Basis: direct: commit 5b508b91 and the three count-bearing surfaces, verifier-confirmed; no prose writeup of this incident exists (confirming the zero-prose-sources premise).

Rationale: The verifier rated this the strongest candidate: a repeatable forensic method demonstrated on a race every team with parallel PRs has lived, days-fresh, and novel against all four published pieces.

Downsides: Short by nature; the method matters more than the incident, so it must resist padding.

4. The Bug That Shipped Twiceprocess-lifecycle

Description: ce-polish-beta's promotion shipped cleanup wiring that was a silent no-op — no error, no log, no failing test — and months later ce-dogfood-beta nearly copied the same broken pattern as its template. Told blamelessly, aviation-report style: immediate cause (one missing switch case) vs systemic cause (a looks-complete precedent nobody regression-tested). The transferable punchline is the test-seeding rule: seed regression tests with the old state, because a test seeded with the current one passes for the wrong reason. Original-antigenic-sin framing optional; graveyard flavor from the STALE_* registries (dead names: ce:brainstorm, git-commit, ce-polish-beta).

Basis: direct: beta-promotion-cleanup-registry-wiring.md:23-26, 102 and legacy-cleanup.ts:89-90, verifier-confirmed verbatim.

Rationale: The most confessional story in the corpus (the register the landscape scan says resonates), four-frame convergence, and it generalizes to any migration/cleanup code anywhere.

Downsides: Adjacent to the compounding article's "failure classes get retired" beat; the twist here is the failure class that almost wasn't retired.

Confidence: 85% · Complexity: Low

5. Overruled: doctrine as common lawauthoring-doctrine

Description: docs/solutions/ is case law; AGENTS.md is statute. The SKILL_DIR saga is the arc: three cases (#764, #811, #898) produced an absolutist rule ("always anchor"), codified per incuriam — without consulting binding authority — then formally overruled when checked against the agentskills.io spec, leaving the current three-tier model with the scar tissue in the text ("a conservative choice, not a claim that bare relative cannot work"). Ends on pending litigation (#944 still open).

Basis: direct: the learning's self-reversal text, verifier-confirmed nearly word-for-word ("wrong turn", "over-generalized", "codified... before it was checked").

Rationale: Everyone writes rules from pain; almost no one documents the reversal. This repo preserved both states of the belief, which makes an epistemology story no generic content can fake.

Downsides: Shares the SKILL_DIR evidence pool with the cut portability piece; keep the focus on doctrine formation, not the bugs.

Confidence: 80% · Complexity: Medium

6. Interlocked: ten collisions on the git railwayhistory-stories

Description: The git-workflow whack-a-mole: ten numbered edge cases in strict causal order, six failed prose fixes, resolved only by redesigning the skill as a state machine. Railway signalling is the historically correct frame — interlocking exists to make conflicting routes mechanically unselectable, the track circuit (git status) detects the train regardless of why it's there, and prose workflows are timetable signalling. Durable reader test: "am I signalling by timetable or by track circuit?"

Basis: direct: git-workflow-skills-need-explicit-state-machines.md — verifier confirmed exactly 10 numbered cases and 6 What-Didn't-Work bullets.

Rationale: The most vivid blow-by-blow debugging narrative in the corpus (Dan-Luu-grade), with a 150-year-old fix — and the whack-a-mole→state-machine arc is the most common failure shape in agent-skill authoring everywhere.

Downsides: Single-frame provenance (no convergence corroboration); the railway metaphor needs discipline to stay load-bearing.

Confidence: 75% · Complexity: Medium

7. You Can't Reopen a Closed Periodprocess-lifecycle

Description: Version drift as double-entry bookkeeping: the release-please manifest is the general ledger, the six version-bearing files are subledgers, and the incident's nastiest state — subledgers agreeing with each other but not the ledger — passes local checks and still regresses the next release. The three recovery paths map to accounting doctrine: correcting entry (forward-sync), editing a closed period (backward-revert, only if nobody installed the drifted version), restatement (release-as pin). Root cause: transactions posted outside the control environment (direct-to-main merges).

Basis: direct: release-please-version-drift-recovery.md:56, 75-76, 101-138, verifier-confirmed. reasoned: Path B's risk is literally the closed-period invariant — a third party relied on the published number.

Rationale: Every team on release automation hits drift and improvises recovery under pressure; this converts a hard-won playbook into a mental model nobody argues with.

Downsides: Thematically adjacent to idea 2 (shared-number truth); if both ship, sequence them apart.

Confidence: 75% · Complexity: Medium
