# Interview Snapshot: user-001

## Summary

user-001 is a clinical analyst with a nurse background at a regional healthcare payer, who previously consulted on and taught a similar analytics platform at another large payer. She had already self-onboarded to the analytics platform — downloading the manual, setting up dashboards, and exploring filters — before this discovery call. Her core task was identifying populations for disease management and case management outreach programs, but she hit a wall: the platform exports conditions as a single comma-separated column, making downstream Excel analysis extremely difficult. Without cost data in the platform, she and her director (user-001b, also on the call) were trying to use condition counts and risk scores as proxies for member severity, but the lack of condition categorization (chronic vs. acute vs. behavioral health) made it hard to produce actionable outreach lists. They are actively evaluating whether the platform can meet their reporting needs or if they'll need to build the capability elsewhere.

## Insights

### Pain Points

> "I compiled all the conditions, and then I separated them all out because I was like, I'm gonna be honest. I mean, I'm not saying this is a mean bad way. This is a mess."
- **Type:** pain-point
- **Topics:** data-export, condition-filtering
- **Context:** After exporting member data, conditions appeared as a single comma-separated column. Manual separation in Excel failed because different members had different condition counts, making column alignment impossible.

> "the chronic conditions aren't separated out. It is on a member profile, if you go to the member... no way to see that in a really, or in a..."
- **Type:** pain-point
- **Topics:** condition-filtering, platform-usability
- **Context:** Conditions are visible on individual member profiles but cannot be filtered or viewed by category (chronic, acute, behavioral health) in list/table views.

> "Why don't they make that, that slider default to one?"
- **Type:** pain-point
- **Topics:** platform-usability, condition-filtering
- **Context:** The condition count filter slider defaults to zero, which includes members with no conditions — a useless default for clinical filtering workflows.

> "That was just a good example of error and use... If that was red, then it would mean to me to delete it."
- **Type:** pain-point
- **Topics:** platform-usability
- **Context:** UI interaction for removing filter values was confusing; color coding didn't match user-001's mental model of what "red" means in a clinical interface.
